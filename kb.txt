Elon Musk was born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa.

He studied physics and economics at the University of Pennsylvania, graduating in 1997.

In 1995, Musk co-founded Zip2, a web software company, which was sold to Compaq for nearly $300 million in 1999.

In 1999, he co-founded X.com, an online payment company that later became PayPal after a merger; PayPal was acquired by eBay in 2002 for $1.5 billion.

Elon Musk founded Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (SpaceX) in 2002.

SpaceX launched its first orbital rocket Falcon 1 in 2008, followed by the successful Falcon 9 rocket in 2010.

In 2012, SpaceX's Dragon became the first commercial spacecraft to dock with the International Space Station.

Musk joined Tesla Motors in 2004 as chairman and product architect; he became CEO in 2008.

Tesla produced its first electric car, the Roadster, in 2008.

Tesla’s Model S won Motor Trend’s Car of the Year award in 2013.

Musk revealed the Powerwall home battery system in 2015.

He announced Starlink in 2015, a satellite internet constellation to provide global broadband.

Neuralink, founded by Musk in 2016, focuses on developing brain-machine interfaces.

In 2015, Musk proposed the concept of Hyperloop, a high-speed transportation system.

OpenAI was co-founded by Musk and others in 2015 to ensure safe artificial intelligence development.

Musk acquired Twitter in 2022 and rebranded it as X, aiming to transform social media.

Tesla operates several Gigafactories for battery and vehicle production worldwide, including Gigafactory Nevada and Gigafactory Shanghai.

SpaceX developed the reusable Falcon 9 rocket and the heavy-lift Falcon Heavy rocket, launched first in 2018.

Musk has been named to Time magazine's list of the 100 most influential people multiple times.

He announced plans for Mars colonization through SpaceX’s Starship project, aimed at interplanetary travel.
